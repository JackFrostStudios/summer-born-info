The review set now includes the original three directions plus three B-adjacent refinements that respond to stakeholder feedback around squarer sections, whitespace-led separation, and more polished header treatment.

Round one

Original directions

These are the first three visual theses used to narrow the conversation before the editorial refinement pass.

Direction A

Civic Clarity

Calm, trustworthy, and service-led. This direction uses structured spacing, strong landmarks, and a polished public-service tone.

Direction B

Editorial Warmth

Gentle, human, and reassuring. This direction leans on serif display typography, warmer neutrals, and generous breathing room.

Direction C

Neighbourhood Signal

Bold, modern, and campaign-like. This direction uses condensed headings, energetic contrast, and a more assertive, action-oriented tone.

Round two

Editorial warmth refinements

These three options stay close to Direction B while varying typography, palette, and header composition for a more focused stakeholder comparison.

B1

Morning Ledger

Crisp navy and parchment with a newspaper-like masthead, serif hero typography, and airy section dividers instead of soft cards.

Open prototype

B2

Hearth Bulletin

Clay and oat tones with a calmer humanist rhythm, a polished utility header, and simple panels defined mostly by spacing and rules.

Open prototype

B3

Sunday Review

Moss, linen, and deep ink with a more literary hierarchy, measured whitespace, and an editorial navigation bar that feels composed rather than app-like.
