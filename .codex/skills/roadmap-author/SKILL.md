# Roadmap Author

Use this skill to turn an unclear initiative into a high-level roadmap that other contributors can review and refine.

## Workflow

1. Clarify the scope at a high level.
2. Probe for missing requirements that materially affect priorities, milestones, or risk.
3. Distinguish confirmed inputs from assumptions.
4. Produce a roadmap that focuses on outcomes, phases, and decision points before detailed delivery work.
5. Leave visible open questions instead of pretending uncertain details are settled.

## Probe For

- Problem or opportunity
- Audience, users, or stakeholders
- Desired outcomes and success measures
- Constraints such as dates, budget, staffing, policy, or dependencies
- Risks, unknowns, and decision deadlines
- Non-goals and deferred scope

Ask concise questions that stay at the roadmap level. Do not jump straight to implementation details unless the user asks for them.

## Output Standard

Default to a document with these sections:

1. Overview
2. Problem Statement
3. Goals
4. Non-Goals
5. Stakeholders or Users
6. High-Level Requirements
7. Milestones
8. Dependencies
9. Risks and Mitigations
10. Open Questions
11. Assumptions
12. Next Steps

## Milestone Rules

- Make milestones outcome-based.
- Give each milestone a short name, objective, target window, dependencies, and exit criteria.
- Prefer three to seven milestones for an initial roadmap.
- Use milestone types such as discovery, decision, delivery, validation, and rollout when helpful.
- Keep detailed task breakdowns out of the roadmap unless the user explicitly wants a delivery plan.

## Quality Bar

- Separate facts from assumptions.
- Make tradeoffs visible.
- Keep requirements high level but testable.
- Prefer a short roadmap that drives decisions over a long roadmap that hides uncertainty.
- If the request is too vague to finish well, return the best draft possible plus the missing inputs that matter most.
